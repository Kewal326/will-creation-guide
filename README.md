# Sahay Will Creator

## Why Create a Will?

Creating a will is one of the most important things you can do for yourself and your family. It ensures that your assets and possessions are distributed according to your wishes after you pass away. Without a will, the laws of your country or state will decide how your estate is divided, which may not align with what you would have wanted.

**Key benefits of having a will:**
*   **Control:** You decide who gets what.
*   **Guardianship:** You can appoint guardians for your minor children.
*   **Peace of Mind:** It reduces the burden and potential for conflict among your loved ones during a difficult time.
*   **Executor:** You can choose a trusted person to manage your estate.

## How to Create a Will

### Option 1: Pen and Paper (Traditional Method)
You do not strictly need a lawyer or a website to create a valid will. In many jurisdictions, you can write one yourself.

1.  **Write it down:** Clearly state that this is your last will and testament.
2.  **Identify yourself:** Include your full name and address.
3.  **Revoke previous wills:** State that you revoke all prior wills and codicils.
4.  **Appoint an Executor:** Name the person who will carry out your instructions.
5.  **List your Assets and Beneficiaries:** Be specific about who gets what.
6.  **Sign and Date:** Sign the document at the end and date it.
7.  **Witnesses:** Most laws require two witnesses to watch you sign the will and then sign it themselves. The witnesses usually should not be beneficiaries in the will.

*Note: A handwritten will without witnesses is known as a "Holographic Will" and is recognized in some, but not all, jurisdictions.*
